Paul Schydlo

"What I cannot create, I do not understand." - R. Feynman

The mind is a fascinating piece of matter. Is it made up of discrete, interacting components or an emergent phenomena ruled by simple laws? Or something completely different?

I am driven by the thought of replicating the processes which allow us to see and understand the world, interact naturally with each other.

At the moment I specialize in view invariant representation learning from monocular video. How do we represent context and recognize intent? How can we teach robots to better team members?
